list all files in a directory:
	``ff 'isfile && depth=1' ``

make a "tree" of files and their directories:
	``ff -n 'print "\t"*depth_from(start), basename'``

find all files that're at least 1 gig or are newer than 10 days ago:
	``ff 'size > 1g || modify > -10d'``

add the suffix `-YYYY-MM-DD` to all files but keep the extension:
	(space is concat operator)
	``ff -n 'isfile && mv(file, dir base "-" ymd_date "." suffix)'``

find files newer than 10 days with the enclosing folder is `log`:
	``ff 'isfile && modify > -10d && basename(parent) = "log"'``

find all files that contain "hello" and "world", possibly on separate lines:
	``ff 'contents =~ /hello/ && contents =~ /world/'``

find the largest folder by its immediate files: (`${}` is run at script end)
	``ff -n '${print maxdir} dirsize > dirsize(maxdir) then maxdir=dirsize'``
